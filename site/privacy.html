What data we collect

We use privacy-friendly analytics that do not use cookies, do not track individuals, and do not collect personal information. Specifically:

  • No cookies are set by our analytics
  • No personal data is collected or stored
  • No data is shared with third parties
  • No cross-site or cross-device tracking occurs
  • IP addresses are not stored

We collect only aggregate, anonymous usage data: page views, referrer URLs, browser type, country (derived from request, not stored), and screen size. This data cannot be used to identify any individual visitor.

Cookies

Our sites do not set tracking cookies. Some pages may store small browser-local preferences or favourites to improve the experience, but this information stays on your device and is not transmitted to our servers.

Third-party services

Our sites are hosted on GitHub Pages. GitHub's own privacy policy applies to the hosting infrastructure. We do not use Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel, or any other third-party tracking service.

Some pages may embed content from YouTube, Suno, or other platforms. These embeds are loaded only when you visit those specific pages and are subject to those platforms' own privacy policies.

External links

Our sites link to external websites. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of those sites. We encourage you to read their privacy policies.

Data from the AI Resource Hub

The AI Resource Hub provides model comparison data, pricing, and benchmarks. This data is publicly available factual information aggregated from published sources. We do not collect data about users of this comparison tool.

A downloadable CSV/JSON spreadsheet of model data is available. Downloading this file does not require any personal information.

Contact forms and newsletters

If you subscribe to a newsletter or contact us, you provide your email address voluntarily. We will only use it for the stated purpose (sending the newsletter or responding to your message). You can unsubscribe at any time.

Your rights under UK data protection law

Under the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018, you have the right to:

  • Access any personal data we hold about you
  • Request correction of inaccurate data
  • Request deletion of your data
  • Object to processing of your data
  • Lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO)

Since we do not collect personal data through our analytics, these rights primarily apply to newsletter subscribers or anyone who contacts us directly.

Children

Our sites are not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data from children.
